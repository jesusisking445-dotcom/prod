Toothache

What Causes Toothache? 7 Common Reasons Explained

📅 May 12, 2024⏱ 5 min read✍️ Dr. Kunle Bello

Tooth pain can range from a dull, occasional ache to a sharp, persistent throb that disrupts your daily life. Understanding what's behind your discomfort is the first step toward relief — and toward knowing when home care is enough versus when you need to see a dentist.

1. Tooth decay (cavities) — Bacteria break down tooth enamel, creating small holes that expose the sensitive inner layers. This often causes sharp pain when eating something sweet, hot, or cold.

2. Gum disease — Inflammation and infection of the gums can cause aching pain around the base of your teeth, often accompanied by bleeding or swelling.

3. Cracked or fractured tooth — A crack may not be visible but can cause sharp pain when biting down, especially if it reaches the nerve.

4. Abscess (infection) — A pocket of pus caused by bacterial infection can cause severe, throbbing pain, often with swelling and fever. This requires urgent dental attention.

5. Sinus issues — Sinus infections can sometimes feel like tooth pain in the upper back teeth due to their proximity to the sinus cavities.

6. Teeth grinding (bruxism) — Grinding your teeth, often during sleep, can wear down enamel and cause jaw and tooth pain.

7. Newly erupting teeth — Wisdom teeth or other emerging teeth can cause pressure and pain in the surrounding gum and jaw area.

ℹ️
If your toothache is severe, persistent, or accompanied by swelling or fever, use our Symptom Assessment tool to check your urgency level.
